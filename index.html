//Through the western gate
Saved
SCENE 2 Drafting

By dusk, the western gate had forgotten it was a gate.

Rain silvered the old ironwork and gathered in the carved letters above the arch—names of kings no one bothered to remember. Elian stood beneath them with the letter pressed inside his coat, feeling the paper’s sharp corners each time he breathed.

The guards did not look at him. That was the first wrong thing.

They watched the road instead, both men facing outward as if the danger lay in the country he had crossed, not the city behind them. Beyond the arch, Valenne rose in terraces of slate and smoke. A thousand windows caught the last of the light.

None of them matched the map.

Elian unfolded his father’s atlas beneath the shelter of the wall. The ink was faded, the linen soft at the creases, but the western quarter was unmistakable: three streets fanning from the gate, a bell tower to the north, the river bending south like a hooked finger.

He looked up.

Four streets. No bell tower. And the river—if he could trust the smell of it—was somewhere behind him.

“You planning to stand there all night?”

The voice came from the gatehouse window. A woman leaned on the sill, her uniform unbuttoned at the throat. Unlike the guards, she was looking directly at him.

Elian closed the atlas. “I may have taken a wrong road.”

“Only one?”
